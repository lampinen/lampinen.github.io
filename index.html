I am a PhD candidate in the Department of Psychology at Stanford University. I am in the cognitive area, and am advised by Professor Jay McClelland. My background is in mathematics, physics, and machine learning. I am interested in flexibility and generalization in human cognition and artificial intelligence. I am particularly interested on zero-shot adaptation, multi-task learning, meta-learning, and the factors that contribute to generalization.

I investigate these issues from perspectives ranging from experiments on teaching people math concepts, to developing machine learning techniques and theory.

For more information check out my CV, publications, and a visualization of my projects!
